Welcome to Odyssey!

Odyssey is a text-based, AI-driven adventure game. To get started, you need to connect the game to an AI model by setting up an API Key. Don't worry if you're not tech-savvy—this guide will walk you through the process step-by-step!

1. Getting a Free API Key (Google AI Studio)

An API Key is a secure password that allows Odyssey to communicate with a powerful AI model in the cloud. Google provides free access to their advanced "Gemini" models, which are perfect for playing the game.

Step 1: Go to Google AI Studio and sign in with your Google account.
Step 2: Click on the "Get API key" button in the left sidebar.
Step 3: Click "Create API key", then select "Create API key in a new project".
Step 4: Once generating finishes, copy the long string of letters and numbers. Keep this secret!

2. Giving Odyssey Your API Key

Now that you have your key, you need to paste it into the game's settings.

Step 1: In the game's main menu, click on Settings.
Step 2: Select the API Settings tab on the left.
Step 3: Change the AI Provider dropdown to Google AI (or whichever provider you got your key from).
Step 4: In the "API Key" box below that, paste the key you copied earlier.
Step 5: Click "Save Settings" to persist your key, then click "Fetch Models".
Step 6: Scroll down and select a model from the dropdown. For Google AI, Gemini 1.5 Pro or Gemini 2.5 Flash are excellent choices. Don't forget to hit Save again!

Interactive Setup Example

3. The "Reasoning Models" Setting

In the API Settings, you will see a toggle for Use Reasoning Model. This is designed for next-generation AI models (like xAI's Grok 4.1 Thinking or Anthropic's Claude 3.7 Sonnet extended-thinking mode) that are designed to "think" out loud before taking action.

Advantages (Why turn it on)

• Superior Game Mastering: The AI acts much more intelligently, actively tracking inventory, health, and complex lore rules accurately instead of just guessing.
• Fewer Hallucinations: The AI creates an invisible "scratchpad" where it plans out the narrative consequences of your actions before it writes the actual story response.

Trade-Offs (Why turn it off)

• Higher API Costs: Reasoning models write "invisible" text while thinking, and you are billed for those generated thinking tokens. Playing with reasoning on can drastically increase the cost of a gaming session.
• Slower Responses: Because the AI is actively "thinking" for 10-20 seconds before generating the final story text, you will have to wait significantly longer between turns.

Supported AI Providers

Odyssey supports several platforms for hosting AI. Depending on whether you want free models, the absolute smartest enterprise models, or complete offline privacy, you can choose the right provider for you.

1. OpenRouter (Highly Recommended)

Ideal For: Accessing hundreds of different models (OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta, etc.) all in one place with a single unified wallet.

OpenRouter is an aggregator that lets you use models from almost any AI company without making accounts for all of them. It offers many entirely free models, and paid models are billed per-token (meaning you only pay for exactly what you use). It is also the most reliable way to get high-quality Image Generation models like Flux.

Website: openrouter.ai

2. Google AI Studio (Best Free Experience)

Ideal For: High quality text and image generation completely free of charge.

Google offers native API access to the Gemini family (Flash, Pro, Experimental). Their free tier allows for plenty of daily requests, making it the most sensible choice for newcomers who want world-class "brain" models without needing to enter a credit card.

Website: aistudio.google.com

3. xAI (Grok Models)

Ideal For: Fast, highly intelligent reasoning models known for being less rigidly censored.

The Grok family of models offer competitive logic and creative writing capabilities. If you prefer the xAI ecosystem, you can plug their API key directly into Odyssey.

Website: console.x.ai

4. LM Studio (Local / Offline)

Ideal For: Gamers with powerful graphics cards who want 100% privacy, zero cost, and no internet reliance.

LM Studio allows you to download open-source models (like Llama 3) directly to your hard drive and run them on your PC. This means there are no API fees and total privacy, but the intelligence of the AI is strictly limited by how much RAM/VRAM your computer has.

Website: lmstudio.ai

5. OpenAI Compatible Custom URLs

Ideal For: Advanced users hosting their own inference server (e.g., KoboldCPP, vLLM, Oobabooga) or using a bespoke enterprise proxy.

If you have an API endpoint that supports the standard OpenAI format, you can route the game's traffic there by specifying your custom Base URL in the settings.

Demystifying Models & Costs

AI APIs are not usually billed via a monthly subscription. Instead, they bill you per fraction of a word, known as a "Token".

Think of 1 Token as roughly 0.75 of a word. You pay a fraction of a cent based on how much text you send to the AI (the prompt context) and how much text it writes back to you. The more you play, the longer the context history gets, and the more tokens each turn costs.

Projected API Costs

  • Free Tier (Google AI Studio): $0.00 forever (subject to daily rate limits).
  • Fast & Small Paid Models (e.g., Claude 3 Haiku / Gemini Flash via OpenRouter): Very cheap! A heavy 3-hour gaming session might only cost $0.05 to $0.15 total.
  • Large "Smart" Models (e.g., Grok 4.1 / GPT-4o / Claude 3.5 Sonnet): More expensive. These are massive brains. A heavy 3-hour gaming session might cost $0.50 to $1.50 depending on how much game history is passing back and forth.
  • Image Generation: Usually a flat rate per image rather than per token. Most cost between $0.01 and $0.04 per image. Having auto-image generation on will add up faster than text generation!

Features & Game Systems

Odyssey goes beyond standard text-generation by integrating numerous RPG systems. Here is how they work:

Presets & IP Lore

What is it? Presets map out existing universes (like Star Wars, Harry Potter, etc.) to immediately jump into a recognizable game state.

How it works: The backend automatically reads a `PRESET_KEY` attached to the universe you selected. When creating a character or playing the game, the AI Game Master is rigorously instructed to adhere to the lore, laws of physics, and character archetypes of that specific universe.

Wikipedia & Fandom Lore Integration

What is it? Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). The AI actively researches the real world or game world to prevent hallucinating incorrect facts.

How it works: If you talk about a real-world politician or a specific Star Wars planet, the system silently pauses before answering, queries Wikipedia (for real life) or Wookieepedia (for Star Wars), and injects a secret summary of its findings directly into the AI's memory. This makes the answers extremely accurate!

Game Codex

What is it? A persistent tracking ledger of all Information uncovered during your adventure.

How it works: As you play, the AI is instructed to note down every new NPC it invents and every Location you visit in a hidden JSON file. You can open the Game Codex (on the right bar below the clock during gameplay) to view a comprehensive list of everyone and everywhere you've been.

Player Menu & Stats

What is it? The hub for your character, detailing physical attributes, appearance, and inventory.

How it works: The Game Master AI manages your physical health, energy, and money dynamically based on the storyline. If you get into a fight, it will secretly subtract health. You can view all these stats, along with anything you've collected or bought in the Player Menu (on the left sidebar below your avatar).

Understanding the Settings Options

The API Settings tabs allow you to deeply tweak how the AI behaves. Here’s what the different neuro-linguistic dials mean:

Temperature (Default: 0.7)

Controls how "creative" or random the AI is.
• A lower value (e.g., 0.3) makes the AI more predictable, focused, and robotic.
• A higher value (e.g., 1.2) makes it wild, highly creative, but sometimes prone to hallucinating nonsense.
• For a balanced roleplay game, between 0.7 and 0.9 is generally ideal.

Max Tokens (Default: 2048)

Determines how long the AI's responses are permitted to be. If you notice the AI frequently cutting its stories off mid-sentence, you need to increase this number. If you only want short punchy responses, lower it.

Top P (Default: 1.0)

An alternative to Temperature. If set to 0.1, the AI only considers the top 10% most likely next words. Usually, you should leave this at 1.0 and just alter the Temperature.

Frequency & Presence Penalties (Default: 0.0)

• Frequency Penalty penalizes the AI for repeating the exact same words over and over. If the AI sounds like a broken record using the same prose, turn this up slightly (e.g., 0.3).
• Presence Penalty encourages the AI to talk about new topics rather than circling back to the same concepts.

System Prompts Tab

This is where the magic happens! System Prompts are the invisible rulebooks handed to the AI before every action.

For instance, if you want the game to be extremely grimdark and punishing, you can open the Game Master prompt and add instructions like: "The world is violent and unforgiving. Always inflict severe consequences for the player's mistakes."
